## Recurring Motifs Worth Designing Around

1. Hierarchy as embodied control  
Collars, rank signals, ritual, constrained movement, and punishment are concrete mechanics in the world.

2. The body as social battleground  
Fertility, mutation, adaptation, gendered duty, and biological selection are central narrative pressure points.

3. Intimate choices under systemic force  
Most critical decisions happen in close, domestic, or interpersonal contexts while reflecting large political/ecological structures.

4. Species and caste interdependence  
Distinct groups carry different labor, risks, and timelines; no single group can fully self-sustain.

5. Sacrifice and continuity  
The text repeatedly frames life as partial transmission: survival through fragments, not intact legacy.

6. Strange tenderness within brutality  
Even in coercive systems, moments of care, grief, companionship, and dignity carry heavy emotional impact.
